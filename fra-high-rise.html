High-rise residential buildings

Proportionate, regulation-led fire risk assessments for high-rise and complex residential premises.

Experience across residential portfolios

We carry out fire risk assessments for high-rise buildings, including purpose-built apartment blocks and complex multi-occupied developments. Our experience across residential portfolios enables us to approach each building with a clear understanding of its structure, fire strategy and management responsibilities.

We recognise the heightened obligations that come with managing taller buildings. Our assessments are thorough, measured and proportionate — delivering clear fire safety guidance that supports compliance while remaining practical and achievable for duty holders.

Compliant, structured & regulation-led

High-rise buildings demand a detailed understanding of compartmentation, fire doors, evacuation strategy, fire alarm systems, smoke control measures and ongoing maintenance requirements. Our assessments follow a structured, regulation-led approach: we identify risks clearly, explain findings in straightforward terms and provide proportionate recommendations that support compliance without unnecessary disruption or cost.

We are fully insured and appropriately trained to assess multi-occupied residential buildings, offering reassurance that your property is reviewed competently and responsibly.

Supporting responsible persons

We work with managing agents, freeholders and property managers across a wide range of residential buildings, supporting them as legislation continues to evolve and scrutiny increases around fire safety in taller buildings. With a focus on life safety and legal compliance, we provide clear reporting, practical recommendations and ongoing support where required.

What we assess in high-rise buildings

  • Compartmentation and fire-stopping
  • Fire doors throughout common parts
  • Evacuation strategy (stay put / simultaneous)
  • Fire alarm and smoke control systems
  • Maintenance and management arrangements

Who this is for: Managing agents · freeholders · property managers · RPs
