Construction sites

Structured fire risk assessments for live construction sites, at every stage of the build.

Specialist training in construction fire safety

We carry out fire risk assessments for active construction sites, ensuring fire safety arrangements reflect the changing nature of works and site conditions. Our team holds specialist training in construction fire safety and understands the additional risks presented by hot works, temporary electrics, combustible materials and evolving site layouts.

We assess fire precautions in line with current legislation and industry best practice, providing clear and practical guidance to support safe site operations.

Experienced in live site environments

We have worked with one of the country’s largest construction companies, supporting projects of varying scale and complexity. Construction sites present dynamic fire risks: temporary structures, phased handovers and contractor activity all require ongoing consideration and structured fire safety management.

Our assessments focus on site setup, emergency planning, alarm arrangements, extinguishers, escape routes and housekeeping standards. Reports are clear, concise and tailored to the stage of construction, so recommendations stay proportionate and achievable.

Keeping teams safe on site

Fire safety on construction sites is about protecting people first and foremost. We recognise the responsibility placed on principal contractors and site managers to safeguard workers and visitors. Our approach is practical and solution-focused — helping you maintain compliance while keeping disruption to works to a minimum, whether you’re managing a small refurbishment or a large-scale build.

What we assess on site

  • Site setup and fire safety management plan
  • Hot works and temporary electrics
  • Storage of combustible materials
  • Alarm arrangements and extinguisher provision
  • Escape routes and housekeeping standards

Who this is for: Principal contractors · developers · site managers
